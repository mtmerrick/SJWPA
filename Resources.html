More Resources About San Joaquin's Water


We understand it can be hard to keep up to date with all the news and information surrounding topics you care about and to root through the plethora of information out there, in order to find what will be meaningful to you. Many of our readers have told us they want to know more than just our self-published information can provide, so we've accumulated some additional materials you might be interested in. Additionally, we have crafted an infographic perfect for sharing throughout your favorite social media outlets.




Living in California’s San Joaquin Valley May Harm Your Health
An editorial from the community news site Water Deeply. This article discusses how some Californians are being forced to question if the water supply avaliable today is actually clean enough for consumption. San Joaquin Valley residents face some of the nation’s most contaminated water supplies yet. Worst of all, many don’t even know if it the water is safe or not, until after repercussions like severe health effects. Those who are the most affected are lower income Latino families. This is all due to the contamination of groundwater supplies and negligence. For these families, Nitrates are one of the most problematic contaminates seeping into the water system. Since the Valley is mostly agriculturally driven, we are forced to seek solutions other than just reducing nitrate usage.


Call To Action: Valley’s Contaminated Water is Like ‘Third World Country’
A news article from local news site The Fresno Bee that looks into how the valley has been combating “3rd world country treatment”. The San Joaquin Valley is one of the most forgotten and mistreated areas of California. Statistics depict that 1 in 4 schools in the valley have been impacted by issues stemming from unsafe levels of nitrates in the waters. Additionally, the residents are actually forced to pay more for water than almost all other regions - in fact families in the San Joaquin Valley pay 10% of their monthly budget just to clean their water. In general, residents and sympathetic officials would like to change the current dynamic and mitigate these issues, but solutions are coming along far slower than anyone would like.


California GAMA Priority Basin Project: USGS Scientific Investigations Report 2017
A comprehensive study done by the US Geological Survey and the California Groundwater Ambient Monitoring and Assessment Program, starting in 2010. This research reports raw data that conclusively finds that the San Joaquin Valley has some of the highest levels of waterborne dissolved solids in the country. This includes potentially hazardous materials such as selenium, rhodium, stronium, and more, in addition to more well-known contaminants like nitrates. Importantly, this article discusses the science behind the little-known fact that many of the most problematic contaminants don't alter the waters clearness. This article also explains the consequences of the natural water recharge from the surface into the groundwater/aquifer. This has lead to fecal matters and other surface pollutants making it into the groundwaters. Runoff from other areas like the Sierra Nevadas have also caused increases in contaminants that end up in surface water reservoirs, further devaluing the potability of the waters.
